Charge Quantization from Phase Winding

⸻

Abstract

We derive charge quantization in Phase Theory as a direct consequence of topological phase winding within admissible phase manifolds. Without assuming electric charge, gauge fields, or symmetry groups as primitives, we show that conserved, discrete charge-like quantities arise from nontrivial winding numbers and holonomy classes of stable phase defects. Quantization follows from topological admissibility constraints rather than from imposed quantum conditions or group representations. This paper replaces the Standard Model’s charge postulates with a phase-native, inevitability-based derivation.

Definition 36.1 (Phase Winding Number)

Let \gamma be a closed admissible loop in phase configuration space around a defect. The phase winding number n \in \mathbb{Z} counts the net phase rotation accumulated along \gamma.

Only integer-valued phase windings are admissible for stable defects.

Reasoning

	•	admissibility forbids such configurations.
