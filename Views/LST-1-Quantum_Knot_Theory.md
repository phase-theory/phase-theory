The electron is the simplest stable particle. In QKT, we model the electron as a Möbius Strip loop—a localized wave that twists 180° before reconnecting with itself.

 * Electric Charge: This twist forces the surrounding substrate field lines to diverge or converge. We perceive this permanent distortion of the surrounding field as "electric charge."
 * Antimatter: A positron is the same Möbius topology but twisted with opposite chirality (Right-handed vs. Left-handed).

 * Tau (n=3): The second excited harmonic. Extremely heavy and short-lived.
3. The Hadron Family: Prime Knots and Braids
While Leptons are single loops, Hadrons (Protons/Neutrons) are composite structures defined by Braiding.
