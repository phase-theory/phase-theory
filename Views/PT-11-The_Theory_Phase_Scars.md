Abstract

This paper introduces the concept of Phase Scars: discrete, quantized residual structures left in the Phase Substrate following irreversible phase transitions. We demonstrate that the universe is fundamentally non-ergodic—it does not explore all possible states—and that apparent randomness in quantum mechanics arises from incomplete access to historical phase constraints rather than intrinsic probability. Phase Scars act as topological memory imprints that bias future evolution, providing a deterministic explanation for measurement outcomes, decay pathways, symmetry breaking, and the arrow of time. This resolves the Measurement Problem without wavefunction collapse, eliminates fundamental randomness, and establishes determinism as an emergent property of phase-history accumulation rather than initial conditions.
