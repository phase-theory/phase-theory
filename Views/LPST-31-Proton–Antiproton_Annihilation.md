Why proton–antiproton annihilation yields pions, not just gamma rays — in LPST terms

1. What the LPST antimatter theorem actually says (precisely)

The theorem states:

Matter–antimatter annihilation releases stored phase circulation into allowed propagating modes.

It does not say:
	•	all annihilations go directly to photons,
	•	annihilation is pointlike,
	•	internal structure is irrelevant.

Gamma rays are one possible outlet, not the only one.

2. The key missing ingredient: compositeness and confinement

A proton is not a fundamental LPST defect.

In LPST/TDQT terms:
	•	a proton is a composite topological bound state
	•	made of confined sub-defects (quark-sector phase endpoints + gluonic holonomy)
	•	stabilized by topological confinement, not just energy minimization

An antiproton is the orientation-reversed composite of the same structure.

So proton–antiproton annihilation is not:

one defect + one conjugate → vacuum

It is:

two composite, confined topological networks interacting.

Inside each proton:

	•	confinement means:
	•	phase cannot unwind freely
	•	energy must be redistributed within allowed topological channels

This is the crucial point.

LPST predicts:

Topological cancellation proceeds hierarchically, not instantaneously.

4. Why pions appear (LPST explanation)

What is a pion in LPST?

A pion is:
	•	a low-mass mesonic topological excitation
	•	a collective phase oscillation of the confined quark–holonomy system
	•	the lightest allowed relaxation channel of QCD topology

In LPST language:

Pions are the lowest-energy propagating modes compatible with confinement constraints.

⸻

Why pions dominate

When proton and antiproton overlap:
	1.	Full topological cancellation is blocked by confinement
	2.	The system sheds excess phase circulation into:
	•	the lightest mesonic channels
	3.	These channels correspond to pions, not photons

Photons are allowed only after:
	•	color/holonomy structure has neutralized,
	•	phase topology becomes trivial enough to radiate EM modes efficiently.

5. Why gamma rays dominate in other annihilations

Compare with electron–positron annihilation:
	•	electron is a fundamental LPST defect
	•	no confinement
	•	no internal holonomy
	•	direct conjugate cancellation

So:
e^- + e^+ \rightarrow \gamma + \gamma

This is exactly what LPST predicts.

So the rule is:

System	LPST Outcome
Fundamental + fundamental	Direct photon release
Composite + composite	Intermediate topological decay
Confined systems	Mesonic cascades
Deconfined systems	EM-dominated radiation
