Abstract

This paper establishes the fundamental limit of Phase Theory and, by extension, of any physically realizable theory of reality. We prove that a universe governed by global phase consistency cannot be both fully coherent and fully self-describing. The limitation is not technological, computational, or observational—it is structural. Any observer embedded within a phase-consistent substrate is necessarily phase-bound and therefore excluded from accessing a complete description of the system that defines them. This result unifies and subsumes Gödel’s incompleteness, quantum measurement limits, observer effects, and cosmological horizons into a single principle: Phase Incompleteness. Reality is not unknowable because it is mysterious, but because it is internally consistent.
