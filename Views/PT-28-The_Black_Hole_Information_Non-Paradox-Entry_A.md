Abstract

The Black hole information paradox is traditionally framed as a contradiction between unitarity, semiclassical gravity, and black hole evaporation. If Hawking radiation is purely thermal, information appears destroyed; if information is preserved, locality or horizon regularity appears violated.

Phase Theory dissolves this paradox by rejecting information as an ontic primitive and replacing it with global phase admissibility as the fundamental constraint. We further introduce the Phase-Crystalline Lemma, according to which black holes correspond to maximally constrained, phase-crystalline configurations of the substrate. In this view, black holes are not repositories awaiting information, but terminal phase structures already saturated at the substrate level. Apparent information loss reflects a transition in phase organization and representational partitioning, not physical destruction or duplication.

1. Introduction

The black hole information paradox has been among the most persistent conceptual problems in modern theoretical physics. Its standard formulation asserts an incompatibility between:
	1.	Quantum unitarity,
	2.	Semiclassical black hole evaporation, and
	3.	Smooth horizon physics.

The resulting tension has motivated extreme proposals: firewalls, remnants, radical nonlocality, and holographic dualities. Despite technical progress, the paradox remains conceptually unresolved.

Phase Theory approaches the problem by questioning a premise common to all formulations: that information is a fundamental physical substance whose conservation is mandatory at the deepest level of description.

⸻

2. The Traditional Form of the Paradox

The paradox arises from the following reasoning:
	1.	A pure quantum state collapses into a black hole.
	2.	The black hole evaporates via Hawking radiation.
	3.	The radiation appears thermal and uncorrelated.
	4.	The final state is mixed.
	5.	Therefore, information has been lost.

This reasoning presumes that “information” is a conserved ontic quantity whose loss constitutes a violation of physical law.

⸻

3. The Hidden Ontological Assumption

The paradox depends on the following assumption:

Assumption I (Information Primacy).
Information is an ontic entity that must be conserved independently of representation, partition, or accessibility.

Phase Theory explicitly rejects this assumption.

Information is not primitive. It is a representational measure of accessible phase correlations under a chosen partition of the global system.

5. Why “Information Loss” Is a Category Error

In Phase Theory:
	•	“Information” corresponds to phase correlations accessible to an observer under a chosen partition.
	•	Changing the partition changes what is counted as recoverable information.
	•	No change in the underlying phase configuration is required.

Thus, “information loss” refers to loss of accessibility, not loss of reality.

Lemma (Partition Dependence of Information).
Information content is not an invariant of the global phase configuration \Phi, but of the representational partition imposed upon it.

Proof Sketch.

	2.	Information measures depend on these decompositions.
	3.	Therefore, information may appear lost under one partition while remaining intact globally.
∎

What has occurred is a phase transition, not erasure.
