Abstract

We introduce Topological Defect Quantum Theory (TDQT), a particle ontology derived from Phase Theory, in which all elementary particles are identified as stable topological defects of a phase substrate, rather than excitations of quantum fields. Particle properties—mass, charge, spin, stability, and interaction channels—arise from discrete topological invariants. TDQT predicts a finite, structured particle spectrum, natural confinement, hierarchical annihilation channels, and the existence of novel neutral topological particles inaccessible to effective field theory. We propose concrete laboratory tests in synthetic phase media that can directly validate or falsify the theory.

1. Introduction

	•	Why is the particle spectrum finite?
	•	Why are some particles stable?
	•	Why does confinement exist?
	•	Why do annihilation products depend on compositeness?
	•	Why do field-theoretic extensions proliferate uncontrolled states?

TDQT replaces the field-excitation ontology entirely, grounding particles in topology, not dynamics.
