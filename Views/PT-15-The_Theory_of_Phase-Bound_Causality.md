Abstract

This paper establishes Phase-Bound Causality as the true foundational constraint underlying all causal structure in the universe. We demonstrate that causality is not a consequence of spacetime geometry, light-cones, or relativistic ordering, but instead emerges from the irreversibility and non-commutativity of phase update operations. Even in regimes where spacetime can be warped, folded, phase-shifted, or spectrally laminated, causal order cannot be arbitrarily rearranged without violating phase coherence. This resolves long-standing paradoxes involving closed timelike curves, retrocausality, delayed-choice experiments, and “time loop” thought experiments, while unifying causality with the update ordering principle introduced in Phase Theory.

Key Result:
