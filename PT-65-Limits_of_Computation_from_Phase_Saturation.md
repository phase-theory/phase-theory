Limits of Computation from Phase Saturation

⸻

Abstract

We derive absolute limits on computation in Phase Theory from phase saturation: the finite capacity of admissible phase structure to support distinguishable, coherent transformations. Without appealing to computational complexity axioms, Church–Turing theses, or energy-based bounds alone, we show that all computational limits arise from the same source—finite phase distinguishability and coherence density. Classical, quantum, and hypothetical post-quantum computation are unified under a single constraint: phase cannot support unbounded logical refinement. Computation halts not because algorithms fail, but because phase runs out of room.
