1) Executive summary (one paragraph)

LST is an ontological–mechanical theory: it asserts that the electromagnetic field itself is the sole substance of reality and explains mass, time, space, and gravity via mechanical wave behavior (trapped light, refraction, propagation delay).
LPST is a relational–topological theory: it treats phase interference as fundamental and derives spacetime, matter, and gravity from correlation geometry, phase stiffness, and topological defects, without privileging electromagnetism specifically.

In short: LST explains what reality is made of; LPST explains how reality organizes itself.

They are not competitors—they are different levels of the same stack.
