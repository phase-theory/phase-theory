Ontology of Phase: Why Φ Is Primitive

⸻

Abstract

We argue that phase (Φ) must be treated as the sole primitive of physical ontology. Matter, fields, spacetime, probability, and information are shown to be emergent structures arising from admissible phase configurations and their global consistency constraints. We demonstrate that any ontology taking particles, fields, spacetime, or information as primitive either introduces unobservable entities, irreducible paradoxes, or explanatory regress. Phase Theory resolves these issues by adopting phase as the minimal, sufficient, and irreducible substrate of physical reality.

⸻

1. Why Ontology Matters in Physics

Physics often claims to be agnostic about ontology.

In practice, it is not.

Every theory assumes something is really there:
	•	particles,
	•	fields,
	•	spacetime points,
	•	wavefunctions,
	•	information.

When ontology is left implicit, paradoxes accumulate.

Phase Theory makes ontology explicit—and minimal.

⸻

2. Criteria for a Physical Primitive

A legitimate primitive must be:
	1.	Irreducible — not defined in terms of other entities
	2.	Observable in principle — via its effects
	3.	Universal — present in all physical regimes
	4.	Non-contradictory — free of internal paradox
	5.	Explanatorily generative — able to produce known structure

We now evaluate candidates.

⸻

3. Why Matter Cannot Be Primitive

Particles:
	•	appear and disappear,
	•	change identity,
	•	depend on reference frames,
	•	dissolve into fields or excitations.

Treating matter as primitive leads to:
	•	particle–wave duality,
	•	creation/annihilation paradoxes,
	•	ontology inflation.

Matter behaves like a feature, not a foundation.

⸻

4. Why Fields Cannot Be Primitive

Fields:
	•	are defined over spacetime,
	•	require infinite degrees of freedom,
	•	lack intrinsic individuation.

Field ontology:
	•	presupposes spacetime,
	•	struggles with localization,
	•	collapses under strong coherence (Paper 76).

Fields describe behavior.
They do not ground existence.

⸻

5. Why Spacetime Cannot Be Primitive

Spacetime:
	•	bends,
	•	emerges,
	•	dissolves near singularities,
	•	fails at quantum scales.

Taking spacetime as primitive:
	•	prevents quantum gravity,
	•	obscures cosmological origin,
	•	conflicts with relational behavior.

Spacetime behaves as order, not substrate.

⸻

6. Why Information Cannot Be Primitive

Information:
	•	requires distinguishability,
	•	presupposes structure,
	•	depends on encoding.

Treating information as primitive:
	•	sneaks in observers,
	•	creates circular definitions,
	•	lacks physical grounding.

Information emerges from phase distinguishability (Paper 58).

7. Why Phase Survives All Eliminations

Phase:
	•	exists without spacetime (Paper 10),

	•	exists without fields (Paper 9),
	•	generates information (Paper 58),
	•	generates probability (Paper 5),

Phase remains when everything else is stripped away.

8. What Phase Is (Ontologically)

Phase is:
	•	relational structure,
	•	ordered variation,
	•	global consistency constraint.

It is not:
	•	an angle in space,

9. Why Φ Is the Minimal Primitive

Phase requires:
	•	no background,
	•	no carriers,
	•	no embedding space.

It only requires:

admissibility and consistency.

This makes Φ the smallest possible ontology capable of generating reality.

We have argued that phase must be treated as the sole primitive of physical ontology. All competing primitives either presuppose deeper structure or generate irresolvable paradoxes. Reality is not made of things.
