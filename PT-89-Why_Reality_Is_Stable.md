⸻

Abstract

We identify the ultimate reason for physical stability within Phase Theory. Stability is shown not to arise from conservation laws, symmetry principles, or dynamical equilibria taken as primitives, but from global phase admissibility constraints that prohibit runaway inconsistency, unbounded distinguishability, and incoherent evolution. We prove that any admissible phase configuration necessarily enforces bounded change, persistence of structure, and resistance to spontaneous dissolution. Reality is stable because instability is structurally inadmissible.
