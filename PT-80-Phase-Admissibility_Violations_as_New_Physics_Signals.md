Phase-Admissibility Violations as New Physics Signals

⸻

Abstract

We define phase-admissibility violations as experimentally observable phenomena that cannot be reconciled with any admissible phase configuration under Phase Theory. Such violations constitute genuine signals of new physics, distinct from anomalies explainable by parameter tuning, environmental effects, or effective-theory breakdowns. We classify admissibility violations, identify their observable signatures, and distinguish them from conventional anomalies in quantum mechanics, quantum field theory, and general relativity. This framework provides a principled criterion for discovery: physics advances not when equations fail, but when phase consistency becomes impossible.

New physics occurs only when no admissible phase configuration exists.

3. What Counts as an Admissibility Violation

Definition 80.1 (Phase-Admissibility Violation)

A phase-admissibility violation occurs when observed behavior cannot be embedded in any globally consistent phase configuration without contradiction, saturation overflow, or topological inconsistency.

This is stronger than:
	•	prediction failure,
	•	model mismatch,
	•	unexplained noise.

4. Classes of Admissibility Violations

We identify five distinct classes.

Class I — Overdistinguishability

Observed distinguishability exceeds theoretical phase limits (Paper 59).

Signature:
	•	information density beyond saturation bounds,
	•	infinite precision behavior.

⸻

Class II — Super-Coherence

Global coherence persists beyond admissibility limits.

Signature:
	•	coherence without entropy export,
	•	coherence surviving past predicted collapse.

⸻

Class III — Topological Inconsistency

Observed phase modes cannot be embedded in any admissible topology.

Signature:
	•	forbidden braiding statistics,
	•	inconsistent boundary modes,
	•	non-integer winding without defects.

⸻

Class IV — Nonlocal Signaling

Phase correlations permit controllable signaling.

Signature:
	•	faster-than-light communication,
	•	causal order violations.

This is strictly forbidden.

⸻

Class V — Entropy Reversal Without Sink

Phase entropy decreases globally without compensation.

Signature:
	•	net entropy reduction without export,
	•	violation of phase thermodynamics (Paper 8).

5. Why QFT and GR Anomalies Usually Aren’t Violations

Most anomalies are:
	•	effective-theory breakdowns,

Phase Theory absorbs many such anomalies without violation.

Only structural impossibility qualifies.

6. Experimental Identification Protocol

To claim new physics, an experiment must:
	1.	Exclude thermal, electromagnetic, and environmental explanations
	2.	Demonstrate reproducibility across platforms
	3.	Show incompatibility with any admissible phase embedding
	4.	Rule out saturation, geometry, and interface effects

This is a high bar—intentionally.

7. Distinguishing Violations from Saturation Effects

Saturation effects:
	•	produce abrupt failures,
	•	appear geometry-dependent,
	•	self-limit.

Violations:
	•	persist beyond saturation,
	•	contradict admissibility structure,
	•	do not self-heal.

Misclassification is the main risk.
