Equivalence Principle from Phase Universality

⸻

Abstract

We derive the Equivalence Principle in Phase Theory as a direct consequence of phase universality—the fact that all stable phase defects couple identically to admissible phase geometry. Without postulating inertial mass, gravitational mass, spacetime curvature, or equivalence axioms, we show that universal free-fall, local indistinguishability of acceleration and gravitation, and composition-independent motion arise inevitably from the way admissibility gradients act on phase structure. The Equivalence Principle is thus revealed not as an empirical coincidence, but as a structural necessity of phase-consistent reality.

In classical and relativistic physics, the Equivalence Principle (EP) states that:

	•	all bodies fall identically in a gravitational field,
	•	local acceleration is indistinguishable from gravitation.

These facts are:
	•	experimentally precise,
	•	conceptually unexplained.

Why should every object respond identically to gravity?

Phase Theory answers this structurally.
