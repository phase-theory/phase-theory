Room-Temperature Coherence Systems

⸻

Abstract

We demonstrate that sustained coherence at room temperature is not exceptional but structurally achievable in Phase Theory through admissibility engineering, phase cooling, and coherence locking. Without invoking cryogenics, extreme isolation, or exotic materials, we show that coherence survives whenever phase entropy is exported faster than it accumulates locally. Room-temperature coherence systems emerge naturally when admissibility geometry prevents decoherence pathways, reframing thermal noise as a routing problem rather than a temperature problem. This paper establishes the general design principles enabling coherent operation under ambient conditions.
