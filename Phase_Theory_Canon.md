The Phase Canon: Internal Consistency, Open Problems, and Closure

⸻

Abstract

We present the closure of the Phase Theory canon by demonstrating its internal consistency, summarizing its completed reductions, and explicitly identifying the remaining open problems that are structural rather than foundational. Phase Theory is shown to be ontologically minimal, conceptually closed, and empirically accountable. No unresolved paradoxes remain internal to the framework; remaining uncertainties correspond to contingent structure, not missing principles. This paper marks the completion of Phase Theory as a foundational physical framework while delineating its legitimate future extensions.

⸻

1. What Canonical Closure Means

Closure does not mean:
	•	final truth,
	•	absence of future discovery,
	•	completion of all knowledge.

Canonical closure means:

All foundational primitives are defined,
all internal paradoxes are resolved or eliminated,
and no explanatory gaps remain within the stated scope.

This paper verifies that Phase Theory satisfies those conditions.

⸻

This inventory is complete and minimal.

7. What Would Break Closure

Phase Theory would lose canonical closure if:
	•	a new irreducible primitive were required,
	•	a paradox reappeared internally,
	•	a phenomenon demanded intrinsic randomness,
	•	spacetime or fields proved irreducible.

No such requirement has been identified.
