Spin, Statistics, and Phase Holonomy

⸻

Abstract

We derive spin and quantum statistics in Phase Theory as consequences of phase holonomy associated with admissible transport of stable phase defects. Without postulating intrinsic spin, spinor representations, or statistical axioms, we show that half-integer and integer spin behavior emerges from nontrivial holonomy under closed admissible loops in phase configuration space. Exchange statistics arise from the same topological structure, unifying spin and statistics as manifestations of admissible phase geometry. This framework reproduces the spin–statistics connection without invoking Lorentz invariance, canonical commutation relations, or quantum field theory primitives.
