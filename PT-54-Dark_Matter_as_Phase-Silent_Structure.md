Abstract

We reinterpret dark matter in Phase Theory as phase-silent structure: stable or metastable admissible phase configurations that gravitate through admissibility geometry but do not participate in radiative or interaction-visible phase modes. Without postulating new particles, weakly interacting matter, or modifications of gravity, we show that galactic rotation curves, gravitational lensing, and large-scale structure formation arise naturally from phase-silent admissibility distortions. Dark matter is not missing mass—it is structural phase content invisible to standard probes.
