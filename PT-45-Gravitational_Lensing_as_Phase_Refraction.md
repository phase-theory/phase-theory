Gravitational Lensing as Phase Refraction

⸻

Abstract

We reinterpret gravitational lensing in Phase Theory as a phenomenon of phase refraction through admissibility gradients, rather than as the bending of spacetime or the action of gravitational force. Without assuming curved spacetime, metric fields, or geodesic postulates as primitives, we show that light-like phase modes follow paths of minimal admissibility cost through regions of varying phase density. Apparent lensing, magnification, time delay, and multiple imaging emerge naturally from refractive phase geometry. This formulation reproduces all known lensing phenomena while relocating gravity’s optical effects to phase-native structure.
