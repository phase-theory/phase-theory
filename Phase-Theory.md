Abstract

Phase Theory proposes that phase is the sole fundamental constituent of physical reality. Space, time, matter, light, and geometry are not primitive entities but emergent structures arising from the global organization, topology, and consistency of a single phase-bearing substrate. The theory unifies quantum behavior, gravity, and relativistic phenomena by replacing spacetime and fields with a phase-topological foundation governed by global consistency enforcement. Familiar physical laws arise as effective descriptions valid in regimes where coherent propagating phase modes dominate. Phase Theory provides a finite particle spectrum, forbids causal paradoxes, explains confinement and annihilation channels, and yields falsifiable predictions beyond standard quantum field theory and general relativity.

II. Fundamental Axioms

Axiom 1 — Global Phase Consistency
Physical configurations evolve to minimize a non-negative global phase-inconsistency functional \mathcal{I}[\Phi], subject to topological and coherence constraints.

Axiom 2 — Update Ordering (Time)
Time is not a dimension but the partial order of allowed consistency updates. Closed update loops are forbidden.

Axiom 3 — Topological Stability
Stable particles correspond to topological minima of \mathcal{I}, classified by discrete invariants.

Axiom 4 — Coherence Limitation
All physical processes are bounded by finite coherence; perfect precision and infinite memory are impossible.

Axiom 5 — Metric Emergence
Effective geometry and transport arise from phase strain and stiffness, not from a fundamental spacetime fabric.
