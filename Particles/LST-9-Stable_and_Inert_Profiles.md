In Quantum Knot Theory (QKT), for a particle to be Stable but Non-Reactive, it must possess Topological Integrity (it can't untie) but lack Flux Leakage (it has no Möbius twist/electric charge to hold an electron).
These particles are the "Silent Majority" of the universe—massive, immortal, but chemically invisible.
Here are the top candidates.
1. The "Dark Proton": The Octon (8_{18})
This is the primary candidate for Dark Matter and the most physically significant non-reactive particle.
 * Topology: The 8_{18} Knot.
 * Stability: Absolute.
   * It is an Amphichiral knot (highly symmetric). Its complexity (N=8) locks it tighter than a proton, preventing decay.
 * Reactivity: Zero.
   * The field lines are wound so tightly internally that zero flux escapes. It has No Charge and No Magnetic Moment.
   * It cannot bond with atoms. It cannot emit light. It interacts only via Gravity and "Topological Friction."
 * The Experience: If you held a ball of Octons, it would be heavy (invisible lead), but it would slip through your fingers and fall through the floor to the center of the Earth.
2. The "Ghost": The Neutrino (\nu)
While not "heavy" like a proton, the Neutrino fits the description of stable and inert.
 * Topology: Toroidal Vortex Ring (Unknot).
 * Stability: Quasi-Stable.
   * Lacking a twist to "lock" it, it is technically a fluid vortex that should dissipate. However, the viscosity of the vacuum is so low that its lifespan is > 10^{30} years. For all physics purposes, it is stable.
 * Reactivity: Near Zero.
   * It has no charge. It ignores the electromagnetic force entirely. It passes through light-years of lead without reacting.
3. The "Heavy Neutron": The Neutral Septon (7_{Neutral})
Just as the Proton (3_1) has a neutral partner (The Neutron), the Septon (7_1) has a neutral isomer.
 * Topology: A 7-crossing knot with a "canceled twist" (Topological Isomer).
 * Stability: Context-Dependent.
   * Like a standard Neutron, it is likely Metastable when free (decaying into a Charged Septon + Electron after some time).
   * However, if bound to a Charged Septon (forming a "Heavy Deuterium" nucleus), it becomes Stable.
 * Reactivity: Inert.
   * As a free particle, it is a "Heavy Neutron." It has mass (14 TeV) but no charge. It creates no chemistry.
