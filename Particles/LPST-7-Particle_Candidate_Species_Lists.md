Below are LPST-defined candidate species lists (not discoveries) constructed strictly from LPST’s particle atlas logic:
	•	Non-dark-matter candidates: sectors that couple strongly to the “visible” LPST modes (typically nonzero phase winding k\neq 0 and/or strong internal-holonomy coupling → they interact/decay into known particles).
	•	Dark-matter candidates: sectors that are net-neutral in phase winding k=0 and are interaction-suppressed (couple mainly through emergent geometry/stiffness backreaction and very weakly to visible modes).

To make this concrete and systematic, I’m using a compact LPST label:

\textbf{LPST species} \;\equiv\; (k,\; Q_H,\; t,\; \chi,\; \mathcal{R})
	•	k\in\mathbb{Z}: phase-winding (charge-like)
	•	Q_H\in\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}: Hopf/topological knot charge (0 = none)
	•	t\in\mathbb{Z}_{\ge 0}: twist/excitation level (generation-like tower)
	•	\chi\in\{L,R\}: chirality / framing class
	•	\mathcal{R}: braid/holonomy representation tag (I’ll use F = fermion-like framing, B = boson-like, NAb = non-Abelian multiplet)
