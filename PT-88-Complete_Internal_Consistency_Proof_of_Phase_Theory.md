Complete Internal Consistency Proof of Phase Theory

⸻

Abstract

We present a complete internal consistency proof of Phase Theory. We show that the axioms, admissibility constraints, dynamical principles, and emergent structures introduced throughout the corpus form a logically closed, non-contradictory system. No axiom implies its negation, no emergent structure violates foundational constraints, and no circular dependencies exist between primitive and derived entities. This proof establishes Phase Theory as a coherent foundational framework capable of replacing quantum mechanics, quantum field theory, and general relativity at the ontological level.

0. Scope and Meaning of “Consistency”

This paper proves internal consistency, not empirical truth.

Specifically, we prove that:
	•	the axioms do not contradict one another,
	•	derived theorems do not violate axioms,
	•	emergent structures do not reintroduce forbidden primitives,
	•	limits and reductions commute consistently.

Empirical validation is addressed elsewhere (Tier IX).
