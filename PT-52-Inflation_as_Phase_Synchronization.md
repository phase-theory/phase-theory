Inflation as Phase Synchronization

⸻

Abstract

We reinterpret cosmological inflation in Phase Theory as a period of rapid phase synchronization and admissibility relaxation, rather than as exponential spacetime expansion driven by a scalar inflaton field. Without postulating new fields, potentials, or finely tuned initial conditions, we show that early-universe homogeneity, isotropy, flatness, and nearly scale-invariant fluctuations arise naturally from global phase synchronization dynamics following Phase Genesis. Inflation is thus revealed as an emergent ordering process within phase space, not a fundamental dynamical epoch requiring additional ontology.

⸻

1. Why Inflation Needs Replacement

Standard inflation explains:
	•	horizon problem,
	•	flatness problem,
	•	homogeneity and isotropy,
	•	primordial fluctuation spectrum.

But it does so by introducing:
	•	an ad hoc scalar field,
	•	an arbitrary potential,
	•	finely tuned parameters,
	•	an unobservable inflationary sector.

Phase Theory already dissolved the horizon problem (Paper 51).
What remains is to explain rapid early ordering without new ontology.

2. What Actually Needs Explaining

The early universe requires:
	•	fast global coordination,
	•	suppression of large gradients,
	•	emergence of weak, correlated fluctuations.

None of these require literal spacetime expansion.

They require global synchronization.

3. Phase Synchronization Defined

Definition 52.1 (Phase Synchronization)

Phase synchronization is the rapid alignment of admissible phase configurations across the system, reducing relative phase mismatch and suppressing large-scale gradients.

Synchronization is:
	•	global,
	•	nonlocal in description (not in causation),
	•	driven by admissibility constraints.

In Phase Theory:
	•	“expansion” is relaxation of admissibility constraints (Paper 51),
	•	early relaxation can occur extremely rapidly,
	•	ordering propagates through admissibility, not spacetime.

The inflationary epoch corresponds to:

a brief period where phase synchronization dominates all other dynamics.

Perfect synchronization is impossible.

Residual fluctuations arise from:
	•	finite admissibility resolution,
	•	topological constraints,
	•	incomplete alignment.

These fluctuations:
	•	are small,
	•	correlated,
	•	nearly scale-invariant.

They are structural leftovers, not quantum noise.

This reproduces the observed spectral tilt naturally.
