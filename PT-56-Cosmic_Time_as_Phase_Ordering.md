Cosmic Time as Phase Ordering

⸻

Abstract

We derive cosmic time in Phase Theory as an emergent measure of global phase ordering, rather than as a fundamental dimension or external parameter. Without postulating time, clocks, or temporal flow as primitives, we show that temporal ordering arises from monotonic changes in admissible phase structure and entropy gradients. Cosmic time is defined by the sequence of phase-ordering transitions that progressively increase distinguishability and stabilize structure. This framework explains the arrow of time, cosmological time coordination, and the universality of temporal direction without invoking special initial conditions or fundamental temporal axioms.

⸻

1. Why Time Cannot Be Fundamental

In standard physics, time is treated as:
	•	a background coordinate,
	•	a universal parameter,
	•	an independent dimension.

This creates deep puzzles:
	•	why time flows,
	•	why it has a direction,
	•	why all clocks agree cosmologically.

If time were fundamental, none of these would be explained.

Phase Theory instead asks:

What makes ordering possible at all?

⸻

2. Ordering Precedes Time

Before time exists, there can still be:
	•	admissible configurations,
	•	relations of compatibility,
	•	constraints on reconfiguration.

Time appears only when:
	•	phase evolution acquires a consistent ordering,
	•	changes can be ranked as “earlier” or “later.”

Ordering is primary.
Time is secondary.

3. Phase Ordering Defined

Definition 56.1 (Phase Ordering)

Phase ordering is the monotonic progression of admissible phase configurations toward states of increased distinguishability, stabilization, and reduced global constraint tension.

Ordering does not require clocks.
It requires irreversibility of admissibility refinement.

4. Cosmic Time as an Order Parameter

Cosmic time is defined as:

a global order parameter indexing the progression of phase ordering.

Formally, cosmic time T_\Phi is any monotonic functional of admissibility structure that:
	•	increases as ordering proceeds,
	•	is globally comparable,
	•	is insensitive to local fluctuations.

Different clocks measure different projections of the same ordering.

5. Why the Arrow of Time Exists

The arrow of time exists because:
	•	admissibility refinement is one-way under coarse description,
	•	ordering cannot be undone without violating admissibility.

Entropy increase is not the cause of time.
It is a symptom of ordering.

6. Why All Clocks Agree Cosmologically

Clocks agree because:
	•	they all couple to the same underlying phase ordering,
	•	different physical processes track the same monotonic progression.

Cosmic time is universal because:

there is only one global ordering of admissible phase evolution.

	•	globally defined,

Time does not flow.

	•	uniform rate of phase ordering under stable conditions,
	•	projection of ordering onto local processes.

Different epochs can have different effective “rates” of time emergence.
