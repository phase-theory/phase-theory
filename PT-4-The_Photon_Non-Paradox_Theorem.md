Abstract

The photon is traditionally regarded as exhibiting irreconcilable wave–particle duality, a status elevated to paradox within standard quantum foundations. We demonstrate that this paradox arises solely from the misidentification of non-primitive constructs—waves and particles—as fundamental entities. Within Phase Theory, the photon is shown to be a non-topological, delocalized phase excitation constrained by global admissibility. We prove that no contradiction exists once phase is treated as ontologically primary. The so-called wave–particle duality is revealed as a category error, and the photon is shown to obey a single, coherent description across all regimes.
