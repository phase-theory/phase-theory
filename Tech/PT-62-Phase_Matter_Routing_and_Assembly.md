Abstract

Phase Matter Routing and Assembly are introduced as a new class of construction technology in which matter is not cut, shaped, transported, and joined through sequential manufacturing steps, but is instead routed and assembled through admissible phase configuration. Unlike classical manufacturing—which relies on tools, tolerances, force application, alignment, fastening, and error correction—Phase Matter Routing and Assembly define final structures as the only admissible configurations of constituent matter. This paper formalizes Phase Matter Routing and Assembly, establishes its dependence on Phase Transportation Systems, Phase Mechanical Infrastructure, Phase Elasticity, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates why misalignment, assembly error, jamming, and rework are structurally impossible. In Phase-Native Engineering, objects are not built; they are allowed to exist only when fully assembled.
