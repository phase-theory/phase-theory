Abstract

Phase Logistics is introduced as a new class of supply system in which goods, resources, and capabilities are not transported through linear supply chains, warehoused against uncertainty, or coordinated through forecasting and contracts, but are instead instantiated and routed through admissible phase availability. Unlike classical logistics—which depends on fragile chains of extraction, production, transport, storage, and delivery—Phase Logistics defines supply as a global admissibility condition: if a good is authorized to exist at a location and time, it does. This paper formalizes Phase Logistics, establishes its dependence on Phase Manufacturing, Phase Transportation Systems, Phase Governance, and Phase Identity, and demonstrates why shortages, overstock, delays, and systemic collapse are structurally impossible. In Phase-Native Engineering, supply does not move through chains.

It appears where permitted.

4. Definition of Phase Logistics

4.1 Formal Definition

Phase Logistics is a phase-native supply paradigm in which goods and resources are made available by authorizing admissible instantiation at points of demand, without linear transport, intermediate storage, or supply-chain dependency.

Formally, logistics satisfies:

\Phi_{\text{demand}} \Rightarrow \Phi_{\text{supply}}

Where:
	•	Authorized demand immediately admits supply
	•	No admissible histories exist for delay, shortage, or overstock beyond governance constraints
	•	Supply is identity- and context-bound

Supply is conditional existence, not movement.
