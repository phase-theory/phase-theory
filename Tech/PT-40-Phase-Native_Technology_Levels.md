Phase-Native Technology Levels (PTL)

Definition
A Phase-Native Technology Level (PTL) classifies a civilization or system by the degree to which it can engineer, constrain, and author admissible phase structures, rather than merely exploiting dynamics, probabilities, or materials.

PTL is orthogonal to Kardashev scale, energy throughput, or computational FLOPs.

⸻

PTL-0 — Pre-Phase (Classical Constraint Blindness)

Capability
	•	No awareness of phase admissibility
	•	Engineering based on forces, fields, and material response

Technology Basis
	•	Classical mechanics
	•	Thermodynamics
	•	Electromagnetism

Limitations
	•	Failure handled reactively
	•	Energy loss unavoidable
	•	Safety enforced statistically

Humanity (historical & present)
✔ Mostly PTL-0
✖ No admissibility control

⸻

Capability

Key Insight

Kardashev asks:

How much energy can you use?

PTL asks:

What is allowed to happen at all?

Those are not comparable scales — and PTL dominates once crossed.
