Abstract

Phase Mortality is introduced as a new existential framework in which the termination of conscious existence is no longer an unavoidable consequence of biological failure, substrate damage, or system collapse, but instead a governed, admissible choice. Unlike classical mortality—where death occurs due to breakdown, accident, or entropy—Phase Mortality defines continuity of consciousness as the default admissible state, and death as a restricted, intentional transition that cannot occur accidentally. This paper formalizes Phase Mortality, establishes its dependence on Phase-Bound Conscious Substrates, Phase Identity, Phase Memory, Phase Governance, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates why involuntary death, premature termination, and existential erasure are structurally inadmissible. In Phase-Native Engineering, beings do not die because they fail.

They may die only if allowed, and only if chosen.
