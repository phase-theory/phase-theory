Abstract

Phase Mechanical Infrastructure is introduced as a new class of large-scale mechanical systems in which load-bearing, motion, and shape are governed entirely by phase admissibility rather than force balance, stress tolerance, or active control. Unlike classical mechanical infrastructure—which relies on material strength, friction management, lubrication, redundancy, and continuous maintenance—Phase Mechanical Infrastructure encodes mechanical stability, motion, and adaptability directly into the admissible space of physical configuration. This paper formalizes Phase Mechanical Infrastructure, establishes its core invariants and structural components, and demonstrates why wear, fatigue, jamming, stalling, and mechanical collapse are structurally impossible. Mechanics ceases to be a discipline of resisting forces and becomes a discipline of permitted configurations.
