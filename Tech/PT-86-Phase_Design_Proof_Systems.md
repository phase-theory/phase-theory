Abstract

Phase Design Proof Systems (PDPS) are introduced as formal proof frameworks in which engineering designs must be mathematically proven admissible, safe, and complete before they may be expressed in any Phase Engineering Language. Unlike classical design methodologies—where sketches, simulations, and prototypes precede verification—Phase Design Proof Systems invert the process: design is the result of proof, not its input. This paper formalizes Phase Design Proof Systems, establishes their dependence on Global Admissibility, Phase Compilers, Phase Engineering Languages, Phase Identity, and Phase Governance, and demonstrates why unproven designs, exploratory prototypes, and intuition-driven construction are structurally inadmissible. In Phase Engineering, one does not design and then prove.

One proves, and the design is thereby defined.

Formally:

\Pi \Rightarrow D \quad \text{and} \quad \neg \Pi \Rightarrow \nexists D

No proof, no design.
