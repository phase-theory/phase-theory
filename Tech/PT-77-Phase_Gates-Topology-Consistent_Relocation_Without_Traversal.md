Abstract

Phase Gates are introduced as a new class of relocation mechanism in which entities are repositioned between distant regions of spacetime without traversal, intermediate states, paths, or transit, while preserving topological, causal, and identity consistency. Unlike classical transportation systems—vehicles, wormholes, warp metrics, or spacetime shortcuts—which depend on motion through space or deformation of spacetime geometry—Phase Gates operate by re-authorizing phase identity continuity across disjoint spatial supports. This paper formalizes Phase Gates, establishes their dependence on Phase Identity, Phase Horizons, Phase Lattices, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates why paradoxes of superluminality, momentum violation, temporal inconsistency, and destructive transit effects are structurally impossible. Phase Gates do not move things.

They rebind where things are allowed to be.

4. Definition of Phase Gates

4.1 Formal Definition

A Phase Gate is a phase-native relocation structure that performs a topology-consistent reattachment of phase identity from an origin spatial support \Sigma_A to a destination spatial support \Sigma_B, without any admissible intermediate phase configurations corresponding to traversal, transit, or path existence.

Formally, for an entity with identity \mathcal{I}:

\gamma(t^-): (\mathcal{I}, \Sigma_A) \quad\rightarrow\quad
\gamma(t^+): (\mathcal{I}, \Sigma_B)

with no admissible \gamma(t) defined for \Sigma \notin \{\Sigma_A, \Sigma_B\}.

Distance is not traversed.
