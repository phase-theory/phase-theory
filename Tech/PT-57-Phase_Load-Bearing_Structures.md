Abstract

Phase Load-Bearing Structures are introduced as a new class of structural systems whose ability to support load is enforced not by material strength, redundancy, or safety margins, but by global phase admissibility constraints. Unlike classical structures—which resist collapse through force balance and material limits—Phase Load-Bearing Structures encode collapse itself as an inadmissible phase evolution. This paper formalizes Phase Load-Bearing Structures, establishes their dependence on Phase Lattices, Phase Metamaterials, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates why buckling, fracture, fatigue, and catastrophic structural failure are structurally impossible. These structures do not hold loads by resisting forces; they exist in configurations where collapse is not allowed to occur.
