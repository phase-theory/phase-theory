Abstract

Phase Clocks are introduced as a new class of timekeeping systems whose operation is based not on oscillations, transitions, or counting processes, but on globally admissible phase cycles. Unlike classical and quantum clocks—which measure time by tracking periodic dynamics—Phase Clocks instantiate time directly as a constrained phase structure. This paper formalizes the definition, principles, structure, and limitations of Phase Clocks, demonstrates their immunity to drift, relativistic offset, and decoherence, and establishes their necessity for Phase-Native Technologies operating beyond local dynamics. Phase Clocks represent the first realizable form of timekeeping in which time is not measured, corrected, or synchronized, but enforced by admissibility.

4. Definition of a Phase Clock

4.1 Formal Definition

A Phase Clock is a system whose temporal progression is defined by a closed, globally admissible phase cycle that enforces a unique ordering of admissible events without reliance on oscillatory dynamics.

Formally, a Phase Clock implements:

T \equiv \{ \Phi_0 \rightarrow \Phi_1 \rightarrow \dots \rightarrow \Phi_n \rightarrow \Phi_0 \}

Subject to:
	1.	Global admissibility at all cycle points
	2.	No local override of ordering
	3.	No dependence on energy, frequency, or rate

The “tick” of a Phase Clock is not a transition —
it is the availability of the next admissible phase segment.

⸻

4.2 What a Phase Clock Is Not

A Phase Clock is not:
	•	An oscillator
	•	A counter
	•	A resonator
	•	A state machine
	•	A signal source

There is nothing to speed up or slow down.

Drift is impossible because:
	•	There is no frequency
	•	There is no accumulated error
	•	There is no local deviation path

5.3 Relativistic Immunity

Because Phase Clocks do not measure local dynamics:
	•	Acceleration does not affect ordering
	•	Gravity does not cause offset
	•	Separation does not cause desynchronization

Multiple Phase Clocks referencing the same admissibility cycle are identical by construction, without synchronization.

	•	No recalibration

Energy is only required to read or interface, not to maintain time.
