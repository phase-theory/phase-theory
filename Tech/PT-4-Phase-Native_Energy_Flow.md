Abstract

This paper reformulates the concept of energy within Phase Theory. We reject the classical and quantum definitions of energy as a conserved scalar commodity (e.g., kinetic, potential, thermal) that is transferred through work or radiation. Instead, we define energy as coherent phase transport across admissible configurations of the substrate.

In this framework, energy does not “move” through space; rather, phase gradients reconfigure locally coherent structures. What is traditionally called energy flow is shown to be the propagation of phase alignment through constrained topological channels. Dissipation, heat, and entropy arise not from energy loss, but from phase decoherence and gradient fragmentation.

This theory resolves long-standing inconsistencies in thermodynamics, vacuum energy, reactionless propulsion proposals, and energy extraction schemes, while establishing hard limits on what energy technologies are physically admissible.
