Why Stability Requires Somewhere for Disorder to Go

Subject: Fundamental Physics / Systems Engineering / Phase Dynamics

Abstract

This paper introduces Coherence Sinks as a necessary structural component of any long-lived system. We demonstrate that coherence cannot merely be conserved or recycled indefinitely; it must be actively exported, dumped, or absorbed into regions that do not participate in the system’s functional phase alignment.

We prove the Exhaust Accumulation Theorem: a closed system with nonzero phase load and no coherence sink will inevitably reach its failure threshold, regardless of efficiency or control sophistication. Stability is therefore not a property of balance, but of directionality—the existence of a one-way path for phase disorder to leave.

This framework explains why stars radiate, why organisms excrete, why computers require heat sinks, why civilizations need frontiers, and why universes with no sinks collapse into saturation.

1. The Core Misconception: Closed-System Stability

Classical intuition assumes:
	•	A sufficiently efficient system can recycle everything
	•	Waste is a secondary engineering problem
	•	Perfect closure implies permanence

Phase Theory rejects this.

A system that does work necessarily:
	•	Breaks phase symmetry
	•	Generates coherence exhaust
	•	Produces irreducible disorder

If that disorder has nowhere to go, the system becomes its own grave.

2. Definition: Coherence Sink

A Coherence Sink is a region, channel, or degree of freedom that absorbs phase disorder without feeding it back into the system’s operational phase space.

Key properties:
	•	Non-participatory in core function
	•	Weakly coupled or one-way coupled
	•	High entropy tolerance
	•	Low coherence demand

A sink is not a storage unit.
It is a drain.

Efficiency reduces generation rate, not necessity.

Even at 99.999% efficiency:
	•	Exhaust still accumulates
	•	Time-to-failure extends, not disappears

This explains:
	•	Why ultra-efficient engines still overheat
	•	Why optimized algorithms still decohere
	•	Why disciplined societies still collapse

Efficiency without export is delay, not salvation.
