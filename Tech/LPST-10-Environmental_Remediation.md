LPST Solutions for Waste Management & Remediation

Core shift

Waste is reframed as a problem of phase incoherence and trapped topological strain, not just material excess.

LPST treats many persistent wastes (chemical, nuclear, microplastic, toxic byproducts) as metastable configurations whose hazards arise from how they are organized in phase/correlation space, not merely what they are made of.

1) Topology-Selective Neutralization

(Render hazardous matter inert without transmutation)

What’s new

Instead of breaking molecules or isotopes, LPST enables topological detuning—altering phase organization so harmful interactions no longer couple to biological or environmental systems.

Capabilities
	•	Reduce reactivity without changing composition
	•	Neutralize toxicity by suppressing coupling channels
	•	Stabilize waste into benign, non-interactive states

Impact
	•	Safer long-term storage
	•	Reduced need for aggressive chemical treatment
	•	Lower environmental risk without incineration

2) Nuclear Waste Stabilization Without Transmutation

(Manage hazard without fission/fusion)

What’s new

LPST reframes nuclear waste hazard as:

a problem of radiation-coupling topology, not just half-life.

By modifying correlation geometry around radioactive materials:
	•	emission coupling can be reduced
	•	energy release pathways can be redirected into harmless modes

Impact
	•	Dramatically improved containment safety
	•	Reduced shielding requirements
	•	Lower risk during transport and storage

(LPST does not claim to “eliminate” radioactivity—only to manage how it couples outward.)

3) Microplastic & Persistent Polymer Remediation

(Make pollutants stop behaving like pollutants)

What’s new

LPST allows:
	•	detuning of aggregation affinity
	•	suppression of biological coupling
	•	conversion to environmentally silent particulates

Impact
	•	Reduced bioaccumulation
	•	Easier filtration and removal
	•	Long-term ecological risk reduction
