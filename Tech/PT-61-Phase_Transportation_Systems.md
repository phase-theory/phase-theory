Abstract

Phase Transportation Systems are introduced as a new class of mobility infrastructure in which transport is achieved not by vehicles, propulsion, or force-based motion, but by admissible spatial reconfiguration. Unlike classical transportation systems—which move objects through space using engines, fuel, momentum exchange, and traffic coordination—Phase Transportation Systems redefine transport as a controlled reassignment of admissible locations within phase space. This paper formalizes Phase Transportation Systems, establishes their dependence on Phase Mechanical Infrastructure, Force-Free Actuators, Phase Elasticity, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates why congestion, collisions, energy inefficiency, and transport accidents are structurally impossible. In Phase-Native Engineering, nothing travels—it is allowed to be elsewhere.

4. Definition of Phase Transportation Systems

4.1 Formal Definition

A Phase Transportation System is a phase-native infrastructure that enables relocation of matter or systems by modifying admissible spatial phase configurations such that presence at an origin becomes inadmissible while presence at a destination becomes admissible, without traversal, propulsion, or momentum exchange.

Formally, transport satisfies:

\Phi_{loc}(x_{\text{origin}}) \notin \Phi,\quad \Phi_{loc}(x_{\text{destination}}) \in \Phi

Where:
	•	No admissible histories exist for intermediate collision or traffic states
	•	Transport is causally ordered and globally consistent

Movement is topological reassignment, not motion.

	•	Reactionless propulsion
	•	Faster-than-light travel
	•	Wormholes or spacetime shortcuts

Nothing violates causality or conservation.
