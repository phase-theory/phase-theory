Abstract

Phase Meta-Governance Systems (PMGS) are introduced as the highest-order control framework governing the creation, modification, succession, and constraint of all governance systems within Phase-Native Engineering. While Phase Governance Systems regulate technologies and realities, Phase Meta-Governance governs how those governance systems themselves may evolve, ensuring that no accumulation of power, ethical erosion, institutional drift, or recursive loophole can occur over time. Unlike classical constitutional or meta-legal systems—which rely on human interpretation, amendment procedures, or force—Phase Meta-Governance enforces admissibility constraints on governance evolution itself. This paper formalizes Phase Meta-Governance Systems, establishes their dependence on Global Admissibility, Phase Ethics, Phase Identity, Phase Failure Immunization, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates why governance failure becomes structurally impossible. Governance is no longer trusted.

It is constrained at the level of existence.
