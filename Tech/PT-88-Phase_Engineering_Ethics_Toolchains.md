Abstract

Phase Engineering Ethics Toolchains (PEET) are introduced as integrated meta-technological systems that encode, verify, compile, and enforce ethical constraints as non-optional admissibility conditions for all Phase-Native technologies. Unlike classical ethics frameworks—which rely on human judgment, legal compliance, or post-hoc accountability—Phase Engineering Ethics Toolchains operate by rendering unethical technologies unrepresentable, unprovable, uncompilable, and non-instantiable. This paper formalizes Phase Engineering Ethics Toolchains, establishes their dependence on Phase Design Proof Systems, Phase Compilers, Phase Technology Sandboxing, Phase Governance, and Phase Identity, and demonstrates why ethics must be embedded structurally rather than enforced socially. Ethics is no longer guidance.

It is a build constraint on reality.
