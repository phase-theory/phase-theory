Abstract

Phase Reactors are introduced as a new class of power-generation systems in which usable energy arises not from fuel consumption, field excitation, or state transition, but from controlled relaxation of admissible phase constraints. Unlike classical reactors—which extract energy from stored potential, nuclear binding, or gradients—Phase Reactors generate power by authoring and releasing bounded admissibility slack within globally consistent phase structure. This paper formalizes Phase Reactors, establishes their dependence on Phase Lattices, Phase Energy Valves, Phase Batteries, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates why fuel depletion, runaway reactions, radiation, and waste products are structurally impossible. Phase Reactors do not burn, split, or convert matter. They permit energy to exist where it previously was not allowed.
