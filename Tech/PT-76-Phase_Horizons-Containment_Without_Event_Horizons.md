Abstract

Phase Horizons are introduced as a new class of containment structure in which regions, processes, energies, or entities are bounded without spacetime singularities, causal disconnection, or irreversible information loss. Unlike classical horizons—event horizons, trapped surfaces, confinement fields, or causal barriers—which rely on extreme curvature, infinite density, or permanent causal exclusion—Phase Horizons enforce containment by excluding inadmissible cross-boundary phase trajectories. This paper formalizes Phase Horizons, establishes their dependence on Global Admissibility, Phase Lattices, Phase Shields, Phase Cloaking, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates why singularities, information paradoxes, escape impossibility, and catastrophic collapse are structurally unnecessary. Phase Horizons do not trap things forever.

They govern which transitions are allowed.
