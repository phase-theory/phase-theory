Abstract

Phase Energy Valves are introduced as a new class of energetic devices that regulate energy flow not through fields, materials, or control mechanisms, but through admissible phase directionality. Unlike classical valves, diodes, and rectifiers—which rely on material asymmetry, dissipation, or active control—Phase Energy Valves enforce one-way energy permissibility by structurally excluding reverse or unstable energetic evolutions from the space of admissible phase histories. This paper formalizes Phase Energy Valves, establishes their dependence on Phase Lattices, Phase Metamaterials, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates how energy flow can be made irreversible, stable, and loss-bounded without violating conservation laws. Phase Energy Valves do not push energy; they decide which directions energy is allowed to exist.

4. Definition of Phase Energy Valves

4.1 Formal Definition

A Phase Energy Valve is a phase-native structure that constrains admissible phase evolution such that energy flow is permitted only along specified directions, while all reverse or destabilizing energy evolutions are globally inadmissible.

Formally, a valve defines a constraint:

\Phi_E^+ \subset \Phi

Where:
	•	Φ is the global admissibility functional
	•	Φ_E⁺ permits only forward-directed energy histories
	•	Reverse energy histories are excluded, not resisted

No dissipation is required to enforce directionality.
