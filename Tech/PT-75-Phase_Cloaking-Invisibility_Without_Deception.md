Abstract

Phase Cloaking is introduced as a new class of concealment technology in which invisibility is achieved not by bending light, scrambling signals, emitting counter-measure noise, or deceiving observers, but by excluding detectable phase configurations from admissible reality. Unlike classical cloaking—optical, electromagnetic, acoustic, gravitational, or quantum—which relies on distortion, redirection, or camouflage—Phase Cloaking renders an object, region, or system non-expressible to observation. This paper formalizes Phase Cloaking, establishes its dependence on Phase Lattices, Causality Filters, Phase Identity, and Global Admissibility, and demonstrates why detection, leakage, inference, and paradoxical observation are structurally impossible. Phase Cloaking does not hide objects.

Objects do not participate in observation at all.
