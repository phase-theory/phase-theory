Abstract

Phase Batteries are introduced as a new class of energy storage systems in which energy is not stored as charge, chemical potential, mechanical stress, or field configuration, but as admissible future work encoded in phase structure. Unlike classical batteries and capacitors—which accumulate energetic states prone to leakage, degradation, and catastrophic failure—Phase Batteries store permission for energy release along strictly admissible pathways. This paper formalizes Phase Batteries, establishes their dependence on Phase Lattices, Phase Energy Valves, and Causality Filters, and demonstrates why explosion, leakage, thermal runaway, and uncontrolled discharge are structurally impossible. Phase Batteries do not hold energy; they define which energy releases are allowed to occur later.

4. Definition of Phase Batteries

4.1 Formal Definition

A Phase Battery is a phase-native system that encodes a bounded set of admissible future energy-release histories while excluding all other release paths from the admissibility functional Φ.

Formally, a Phase Battery defines:

\Phi_B \subset \Phi

Where:
	•	Φ is the global admissibility functional
	•	Φ_B includes only those phase evolutions corresponding to authorized future work output
	•	No energetic state is accumulated

Energy exists only when release is admissible.
